About

The Capital of Spain

Located in the heart of Spain is one of the most progressive, modern and beautiful cities of Europe, Madrid. It has a vibrant local arts culture and boisterous nightlife, along with constant reminders of the city’s rich history. It is deservedly renowned as one of the continent’s best tourist destinations.

Museums

Museo Nacional del Prado

Paseo del Prado, s/n, 28014 Madrid, Spain

The Prado Museum is the main Spanish national art museum, located in central Madrid. It is widely considered to have one of the world's finest collections of European art, dating from the 12th century to the early 20th century.

While studying in Madrid, I took a History of Art class that included trips to the Prado every week. I found that every visit brought me a greater sense of appreciation and enjoyment than the last.

Serolla Museum

Paseo del General Martínez Campos, 37, 28010 Madrid, Spain

Joaquin Sorolla was an Impressionist from the city of Valencia and his former studio-mansion in Madrid has been converted into a museum reproducing perfectly a typical early 20th century house. Located just two blocks away from my University, this hidden mansion is flourishing with a beautiful garden and an array of colorful paintings. Admission is very cheap, in fact, some days it is free!

Restaurants

La Barraca

Calle Reina, 29, 28004 Madrid, Spain

Opened in 1935, its name evokes its Valencian essence which is also perceived in its decoration, typical of a house in the Levante region. Clients come here looking to try any of the twelve ways in which they prepare rice. When it comes to paella, La Barraca is my favorite restaurant to bring family and friends to.

Restaurante Botin

Calle de Cuchilleros, 17, 28005 Madrid, Spain

Botín Restaurant, which was founded in 1725, is the oldest in the world according to the Guinness Book of Records and one of the touchstones of Madrid’s traditional cuisine. Over the years it has won numerous awards and recognitions.
